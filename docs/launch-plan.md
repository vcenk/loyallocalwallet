# Launch Plan — Local Loyalty Wallet

## 1. Launch strategy

Start local and manual. The goal is not a big public launch first. The goal is to prove that small businesses will actually use the scanner and that customers will save the wallet pass.

Best first market: Greater Vancouver local businesses.

Best first niches:

- Café
- Bubble tea shop
- Bakery
- Barber
- Nail salon
- Pet groomer

## 2. Pilot business criteria

Choose businesses that:

- Have repeat customers
- Have a counter or front desk
- Are owner-operated or manager-operated
- Are not locked into a complicated POS loyalty program
- Have staff who can use a phone scanner
- Care about customer retention

Avoid first:

- Very large restaurants
- Chains with corporate systems
- Businesses with low repeat frequency
- Businesses that require deep POS integration immediately

## 3. Pilot offer

Suggested pilot offer:

> I’m launching a simple Apple/Google Wallet loyalty system for local businesses. Customers scan a QR code, save your reward card, and staff stamp it by phone. I’ll set it up free for 60 days in exchange for feedback.

Offer:

- Free setup
- Free QR counter sign
- Free 60-day pilot
- Then discounted plan
- Cancel anytime

Ask for:

- feedback call
- testimonial if successful
- permission to use anonymized metrics

## 4. Pilot setup checklist

For each business:

- [ ] Business profile created
- [ ] Logo uploaded
- [ ] Location added
- [ ] Loyalty rule selected
- [ ] Reward text approved
- [ ] Staff invited
- [ ] Staff app installed
- [ ] QR poster printed
- [ ] Test customer enrolled
- [ ] Test stamp added
- [ ] Test reward redemption completed
- [ ] Owner shown dashboard

## 5. Suggested reward templates

- Buy 9 drinks, get 1 free
- Double stamp Tuesday

- Bring a friend: both get a bonus stamp

### Barber

- Refer a friend, both get bonus stamp

### Nail salon

- Birthday bonus
- Weekday slow-time bonus

### Pet groomer

- 5 grooms, get $20 off
- Referral bonus

## 7. Outreach message

Short version:

```txt
Hi [Name], I’m building a simple loyalty wallet for local businesses in Vancouver.

Your customers scan a QR code, save your reward card to Apple/Google Wallet, and your staff stamps it with a phone. No customer app needed.

I’m offering free setup + 60 days free for a few pilot businesses. Would you like me to show you a 5-minute demo?
```

## 8. Pilot success metrics

Track per business:

- QR scans
- Enrolled customers
- Wallet saves
- Stamps issued
- Redemptions
- Returning customers
- Staff scan errors
- Time to stamp
- Owner satisfaction
- Customer complaints/confusion

Minimum success target after 30 days:

- 50+ enrolled customers for café/bubble tea
- 20+ enrolled customers for salon/barber
- Staff can use scanner without help
- At least 10% of enrolled customers get a second stamp

## 9. Feedback questions

Ask owner:

- Was setup easy?
- Did customers understand it?
- Did staff use it correctly?
- What slowed you down?
- What feature did you expect but not see?
- Would you pay CAD $19/month? CAD $39/month?
- What would make this a must-have?

Ask staff:

- Was scanning fast enough?
- Were buttons clear?
- Any customer issues?
- What mistake could happen easily?

Ask customers:

- Was saving the card easy?
- Did you understand the reward?
- Would you use this again?

## 10. Launch phases

### Private alpha

1–2 test businesses, manual support, fix core bugs.

### Local pilot

5–10 businesses in one city/niche.

### Public beta

Landing page, self-serve signup, Stripe trial.

### Niche launch

Focus on one niche, e.g. cafés and bubble tea shops.

## 11. Do not launch with these claims

Avoid:

- “Guaranteed to increase revenue”
- “Get more Google reviews with rewards”
- “Fully automated social sharing verification”
- “Works with all POS systems”
- “Unlimited push notification marketing” (wallet messages are card updates, not free-form push)

Use:

- “Helps bring customers back”
- “No customer app required”
- “Simple wallet loyalty cards”
- “Staff scanner app included”
- “Win-back suggestions for inactive customers”
